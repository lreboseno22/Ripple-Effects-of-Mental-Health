The Foundation

What does mental health mean to you?

Mental Health is not talked about enough as it should be! It is probably one of the most important things every human being should be aware of as it is the foundation of our life. Without our minds being right, it can cause us to abuse our family members or loved ones, poor financial decisions or even do something as serious as hurt ourselves or others physically or mentally.

Many young people in our community between the ages of 10 and 19 do not have consistent access to reliable mental health support, qualified providers, or safe spaces to process their emotional and behavioral needs. Without timely access to mental health care, young people may struggle to stay in school, maintain healthy relationships, and reach their full potential. Addressing the mental health access gap is essential for helping youth build resilience, stability, and long-term well-being.

The Ripple Effect:

Housing Instability

Serious mental health illness can impair a person's ability to maintain a steady income, leading to housing stress, eviction, and unstable shelter. Data shows that individuals with severe behavioral health needs are 1.5 times more likely to experience homelessness. Once unhoused, navigating mental healthcare without a fixed address becomes extraordinarily difficult.

The Legal Pipeline

Across NYC, an estimated 75% of youth currently navigating the juvenile justice system have an active, diagnosed mental health condition. Approximately 15% of the annual jail population reports experiencing homelessness in the year prior to their arrest. With background-checked rental housing, this traps them in a cycle of homelessness and re-arrest

Food Insecurity

Missing regular meals can drive youth into "survival mode." This spikes behavioral crises, increasing emergency police contacts, arrests, and deeper jail system involvement. Roughly half of food-insecure NYC youth report high anxiety, stress, or depressive symptoms. They have a 55% higher rate of mental health and substance use care utilization.
